Introduction

The first stage of alcoholic liver disease is hepatic steatosis, which involves the accumulation of small fat droplets under liver cells approaching the portal tracts. More advanced disease is characterized by marked steatosis, hepatocellular necrosis, and acute inflammation, known as alcoholic hepatitis. There is a need for more effective treatment of alcoholic liver disease as the severe form of the disease is life-threatening. This activity reviews the evaluation and management of alcoholic liver disease and highlights the role of the interprofessional team in the recognition and management of this condition.

Histologic Stages

  • Alcoholic Fatty Liver or Steatosis: Fat accumulates in the liver parenchyma.
  • Alcoholic Hepatitis: Inflammation of liver cells; managed with abstinence, nutrition, treatment of infection, and possibly prednisolone.
  • Alcoholic Cirrhosis: Irreversible liver damage leading to complications.

Pathophysiology

  • Alcohol is metabolized by Alcohol Dehydrogenase and Aldehyde Dehydrogenase.
  • Alcohol dehydrogenase converts alcohol to acetaldehyde, then to acetate by aldehyde dehydrogenase.
  • High NADH production causes fat accumulation (triglycerides) in liver.
  • Immune response leads to inflammation → hepatitis → cirrhosis with continuous alcohol intake.

Physical Examination Signs

  • Enlarged liver/spleen
  • Gynecomastia (excess breast tissue in men)
  • Abdominal swelling (fluid)
  • Red palms, spider angiomas
  • Small testicles
  • Abdominal wall vein dilation
  • Jaundice (yellow skin/eyes)

Tests

  • Complete Blood Count (CBC)
  • Liver Function Tests
  • Coagulation Studies
  • Liver Biopsy

Treatment - Lifestyle Changes

  • Stop alcohol consumption
  • Low-salt, healthy diet
  • Vaccination for Hep A, Hep B, Flu, Pneumococcal
  • Consult doctor about all medications

Conclusion

Naturopathic approaches, including dietary changes, yoga, and hydrotherapy, can be complementary to conventional treatments for alcoholic liver cirrhosis. While complete abstinence from alcohol is crucial, naturopathy can help manage symptoms, improve liver function, and enhance overall well-being.
